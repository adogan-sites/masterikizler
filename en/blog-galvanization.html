What is Galvanization ?

  • Home
  • What is Galvanization ?

    General Description

    • Galvanized, one of the most important materials in construction, stands out with its advantageous structure. Galvanizing is often preferred for prefabricated structures and containers, as it increases the earthquake safety of the building. However, galvanic coating is not only in the construction industry, but also in many other areas.

    What is Galvanization?

    • In its most basic sense, galvanization appears as a metal coating process. In order to carry out this process, the steel must be coated by immersing it in molten zinc at approximately 450 degrees. In this way, while increasing the durability of the metal, surface rusting is prevented. In short, it is ensured that the metal has a long life. The use of galvanization, invented by Luigi Galvani, dates back to the 18th century. Galvanized, which was preferred in those years to prevent corrosion of the metal, has been further developed and started to be used in many areas today.

    What is the process of Galvanizing?

      There are basically 2 different methods for this process, which is also referred to as galvanized coating. However, before these methods, it is necessary to look at the stages of galvanization application. This process takes place in the following stages:
  • Caustic Cleaning
  • Rinsing
  • Pickling
  • Rinsing
  • Flux solution
  • Drying
  • Zinc Bath
  • Cooling and inspection
      • Galvanized coating methods used for different purposes are as follows:
  • 1. Electro galvanizing method
    • Zinc is connected to the anode from these electrodes, called anode and cathode, with two different electrodes. On the other hand, the coating material adheres to the cathode. In this process, an electric current is applied and the anode electrode sticks to the cathode. For this reason, zinc performs the galvanic coating process by sticking the electrode removed from the anode to the material to which the cathode is attached. This process is done with pure zinc and the coating is not removed.
  • 2. Hot Dip Process
    • The hot dip method is the most commonly used galvanizing process. Therefore, for the answer to the question of what is galvanization, the hot dip method is expressed. This method, which increases the life of steel and iron, does not require any maintenance after application. The biggest problem of the hot dip method is that it cannot be welded. In addition, it should be noted that unlike the electro galvanized coating method, it is not applied with pure zinc, but with alloy zinc.

    • What are the Properties of Galvanized?

  • 1.Durable and Long Lasting
    • He said that the galvanizing process is done by melting the zinc at about 50 degrees. Zinc is an element that strengthens the structure of materials. The resulting structure is very strong and not easily deformed. Electroplated metal is much more durable and long-lasting. Galvanizing, which increases the earthquake resistance especially in prefabricated buildings, also contributes to the durability and strength of the structure. In addition, since galvanizing is done at high temperatures, it has a heat-resistant structure. Therefore, it is the preferred product in various sectors. However, galvanization also has a certain lifespan. In contact with excessively acidic or basic substances, the zinc coating will shorten its life.
  • 2.Maintenance Free
    • Another feature of galvanized steel electroplated coating is that it does not require maintenance after application. Therefore, it offers many years of use. On the other hand, the painting process and other coatings have no galvanizing effect. Such applications therefore require much more frequent maintenance than galvanizing.
  • 3.Economical
    • Galvanic is a durable material and also inexpensive. Galvanizing is a much better alternative to painting and allows for a predictable cost calculation. This method does not require any additional costs.
  • 4.Provides protection against corrosion
    • Corrosion; It is called corrosion of metals and metal alloys due to environmental conditions or their unique structure. Humidity, polluted air and acidic or basic environments are among the biggest factors causing corrosion of metallic materials. Corrosion formation is easily prevented by galvanic coatings that provide cathodic protection. Therefore, the life of the metal is extended and its durability is greatly improved.
  • 5.Prevents Rust
    • Galvanizing Worker We have said that galvanizing prevents corrosion. Rust is also a stage of corrosion. Corrosion is a form of oxidation and rust is a stage of this condition. It should be noted that rusty materials are extremely harmful to health. However, using electroplating can prevent the metal from rusting and greatly increase its durability.
  • 6.Saves time
    • Electroplating is a fast process. Surface preparation, painting and inspection do not require time and effort. After the structure is assembled, it can be ready for use or proceed to the next construction phase.
  • 7.Environmentally friendly
    • Galvanized is a material that does not contain any substances harmful to nature. Galvanized steel can be recycled like any other steel product. Zinc used for hot dip galvanizing is suitable for recycling. Therefore, galvanization is seen as an environmentally friendly material that does not leave any residue in nature.
  • 8.Does not contain harmful insects
    • Galvanized has insect repellent properties. Galvanized steel does not contain harmful pests. For this reason, galvanization process emerges as an insect repellent material.
  • 9. Provides Holistic Coverage
    • Galvanized Sheet, the entire surface of the material, including the closed areas, is covered with zinc coating at the factory. Coated steel products are therefore optimally protected inside and out, even at corners and edges. Galvanizing, which is widely used in the construction industry, is a very profitable material. It is one of the indispensable tools of the construction industry with its rust-proof structure. On the other hand, how galvanizing is applied is very important.
